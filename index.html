Audio Samples from "Leveraging Diffusion Probabilistic Modeling and Cross-speaker Style Transfer for Expressive Speech Synthesis"

Abstract: Modeling the diverse styles of speech is the key to expressive speech synthesis. Speech style representations often rely on discrete categories to describe style variation. However, in real-world situations, speech styles are expressed in more than just labeled categories. In this paper, we propose a multi-style speech synthesis method based on the diffusion model to explore the possibility of associating text input with speech styles. The method generates stylized speech that matches the input text by modeling speech styles as potential variables through a diffusion model, without additional stylized reference speech. Initially, a style encoder is pre-trained using unlabeled multi-style speech data. Guided by the style extractor, a diffusion model is adopted to build the relationship between speech style and text input. Experimental results show that the proposed method is effective in both objective and subjective evaluation metrics, which improves the model’s ability to express speech styles.
